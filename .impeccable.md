## Design Context

### Users
Israeli service business owners (freelancers, consultants, coaches, tradespeople) who sell services directly — not executives, not corporate. They're in the field, on calls, trying to close deals. They browse on mobile, likely between meetings. They're skeptical of hype but hungry for a practical edge.

### Brand Personality
Direct, gritty, high-performance. Like a seasoned practitioner handing you their playbook — not a course creator selling a lifestyle. The tone is peer-to-peer: "this is what actually works, here's the system I use."

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark & high-energy. Lead with the dark hero. Orange-gold primary pops against the dark backgrounds. High contrast, confident typography. Not polished-agency, not bro-marketing — more like a tactical field manual or a boxing gym's performance system.

**Anti-references:** No corporate/enterprise energy, no generic course platform look, no cookie-cutter AI-built layout (identical section after section of icon + heading + 3 bullets).

**Theme:** Primarily dark — the dark sections should feel dominant, not like accents. Light sections exist for contrast and breathing room, not as the default.

### Design Principles
1. **No fluff, no filler** — Every element must earn its place. If a section structure repeats identically, break it.
2. **Confidence through asymmetry** — Left/right-aligned content (text-start in RTL) feels more assured than centered-everything.
3. **Rhythm through contrast** — Tight, dense sections followed by generous open ones. Not the same padding everywhere.
4. **Stats are evidence, not decoration** — Numbers embedded in prose feel more credible than isolated "hero metric" cards.
5. **Dark sections are first-class** — This isn't a light page with a dark hero. Dark and light carry equal structural weight.
